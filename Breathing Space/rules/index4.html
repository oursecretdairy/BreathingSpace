Make this your own

This doesn't have to stay here.

You can take this structure anywhere. It can live in a note, on your phone, or in the small second before a door opens.

01

The form

Use the two-line breath.

Take a breath before you ______
You don't have to ______
  1. Start right before entering. Begin at the moment before going into some place, room, or entryway.
  2. Keep it about one moment. Do not make it general. Do not turn it into a whole story.
  3. Follow the format. Keep the breath in two lines so it stays small enough to use.
  4. Keep it short. Let the sentence be something someone can carry with them.
  5. Keep it or leave it anywhere. You can hold onto it, place it somewhere, or pass it on.

02

Forkable object

The project grows through interpretation.

The project grows through how you interpret it and share it. A fork can belong to another room, group, language, hallway, class, neighborhood, website, note, or doorway.

  1. Take the frame. Keep the breath structure, but let the setting change.
  2. Name your threshold. Decide where the pause begins and who might need it there.
  3. Change the pressure. Fill the blank with the thing people feel they have to do: perform, explain, impress, belong, answer, or prove.
  4. Share without closing it. Let other people adapt the system again instead of treating your version as the final one.

03

Door kit

Take the instructions into the real world.

Download a one-page Breathing Space sheet for a door, hallway, classroom, studio, or shared room. It gives someone the prompt, the rules, and a small invitation to leave a pause where they are.

Print it, tape it up, or leave copies nearby so the system can keep living outside the site.
